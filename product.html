Mini Cooper

The BMW MINI Cooper is available in either a 2-door hatchback or a convertible. While both are available in Base and S versions, only the hatchback is available in the new John Cooper Works GP edition, which incorporates more features, a stronger engine, and a sportier ride. There are numerous styling and badging differences between the other two models.



Introduced in 2002 as a completely new design, the BMW MINI Cooper is a throwback to its ancestor of the same name, mimicking its appearance and performance. Whilst the car has been criticized for its poor space-efficiency compared with the original Mini (despite its significantly larger size), its faithfulness to the original's razor sharp handling characteristics and its unique styling has seen the BMW MINI become a sales success in Europe and (since its introduction in 2002) in the U.S. BMW has received a significant backlog of orders throughout the life of the car and the Cowley factory is working at its full capacity of around 100,000 cars per year; all of this with little conventional advertising.
